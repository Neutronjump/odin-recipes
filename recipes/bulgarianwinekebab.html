Bulgarian Wine Kebab

Image credit goes to Nadia Melkowitz

Description

An old Bulgarian recipe for meat slowly cooked in a wine sauce. It takes less than an hour to make and the meat is as tender as if it cooked all day. Either white or red wine can be used depending on preference. You will need a pot with a lid.

Ingredients

Makes 4-6 servings

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Bay leaf
  • Salt & pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon cayenne pepper/paprika
  • 1 tablespoon tomato sauce
  • 8 ounces wine
  • 2 tablespoons flour
  • 8 ounces water
  • 1 large onion
  • 1 kg pork tenderloin, cut into 1 ½ inch cubes
  • Chopped parsley to taste

Steps

  1. Heat up oil in a pot and brown the pork on medium heat.
  2. Add onions and salt, lower heat to low and cook for about 10 minutes, then add the cayenne pepper and stir to coat evenly.
  3. Add the tomato sauce mixed with the water. Add the black pepper and the bay leaf. Cover and continue cooking for 15 minutes.
  4. Lastly, add the flour mixed with the wine, cover and continue to cook for another 10 minutes.
  5. When done, sprinkle with parsley and serve with mashed potatoes or rice as desired.
  6. Extra step! Don't forget to take out the bay leaf before serving.
